USA Soccer Guide (MLS)

An easy, beginner-friendly overview of soccer in the United States, covering rules, MLS format, scoring, and player roles.

1. Introduction to Soccer in the USA

Soccer, known globally as football, is the world's most popular sport and has seen explosive growth in the United States over the last few decades. The game is celebrated for its fluid play, minimal stoppages, and the passionate culture of its fans. In the U.S., Major League Soccer (MLS) represents the highest level of professional play, attracting talent from all over the globe.

A standard match lasts 90 minutes, divided into two 45-minute halves. Unlike many American sports, the clock counts upward and does not stop for minor interruptions. Instead, the referee adds "stoppage time" at the end of each half to account for delays.

2. The Rules of the Game

While the concept is simple—kick the ball into the net—the rules governing play are strict:

  • No Hands: With the exception of the goalkeeper (who must be inside their own penalty box), players may not use their hands or arms to touch the ball. The game is played primarily with the feet, head, and chest.
  • Throw-ins: When the ball crosses the sideline, play is restarted with a throw-in. The player must keep both feet on the ground and throw the ball with two hands from behind the head.
  • Corner Kicks: Awarded when the defending team touches the ball last before it crosses their own goal line. The attacking team kicks the ball from the corner arc.
  • Goal Kicks: Awarded when the attacking team touches the ball last before it crosses the opponent's goal line. The goalkeeper restarts play from the 6-yard box.

Understanding Offside

The offside rule is often the most confusing for new fans. A player is in an offside position if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent (usually the last defender, as the goalkeeper is the last) when the ball is played to them. Being offside is not an offense in itself; it becomes a foul only if the player becomes involved in active play.

3. Scoring and Winning

A goal is worth one point. It is scored when the whole of the ball passes over the goal line, between the goalposts and under the crossbar. The team with the most goals at the end of the match wins. In league play, matches can end in a draw (tie), earning each team one point in the standings. In knockout tournaments, ties are resolved via extra time or penalty shootouts.

4. Player Positions

A standard team consists of 11 players, typically arranged in a formation (e.g., 4-4-2 or 4-3-3):

  • Goalkeeper (GK): The only player allowed to use their hands. Their job is to stop shots and organize the defense.
  • Defenders (Backs):
    • Center Backs (CB): Protect the central area in front of the goal.
    • Full Backs (LB/RB): Defend the wide areas and often support attacks.
  • Midfielders: The engine of the team. They link defense and attack.
    • Defensive Midfielders (CDM): Screen the defense and break up opponent plays.
    • Attacking Midfielders (CAM): Create scoring chances and assist forwards.
    • Wingers: Play on the sidelines, using speed to cross the ball into the box.
  • Forwards (Strikers): The primary goal scorers. They play closest to the opponent's goal and focus on finishing chances.

5. MLS League Structure

Major League Soccer operates differently than many European leagues:

  • Conferences: Teams are divided into the Eastern and Western Conferences to minimize travel and build regional rivalries.
  • Regular Season: Teams play 34 games. The team with the best overall record wins the Supporters' Shield.
  • MLS Cup Playoffs: Unlike Europe where the league winner is the champion, MLS uses a post-season tournament. The top teams from each conference compete in an elimination bracket.
  • MLS Cup: The championship final match that determines the league champion for the season.

6. Major Events

Beyond the regular season, U.S. soccer fans follow several key competitions:

  • U.S. Open Cup: A historic tournament open to all professional and amateur teams in the country.
  • Leagues Cup: A massive tournament featuring all teams from MLS and Mexico's Liga MX.
  • All-Star Game: A mid-season exhibition often featuring the best MLS players against a top international club or league selection.
